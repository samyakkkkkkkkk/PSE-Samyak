Traffic Rules

Learn about the latest traffic rules and regulations to ensure road safety and avoid penalties.

  • Drivers must always keep their vehicle on the left side of the road.
  • Use proper indicators and hand signals when turning.
  • Follow all traffic lights and road signs strictly.
  • Maintain a safe distance between vehicles to prevent collisions.
  • Honking unnecessarily is prohibited in residential and hospital areas.

  • Pedestrians must walk only on pavements and designated walkways.
  • Always cross roads at designated zebra crossings.
  • Do not jaywalk in traffic-congested areas.
  • Wait for the pedestrian signal before crossing at intersections.
  • Use footbridges or underpasses where available.

üöó Speed Limits

  • Urban Areas: Maximum 50 km/h.
  • Highways: Maximum 80 km/h.
  • School Zones: Maximum 20 km/h.
  • Residential Areas: Maximum 30 km/h.
  • Heavy vehicles must maintain lower speed limits in city areas.

üö´ Prohibited Actions

  • Using mobile phones while driving is strictly prohibited.
  • Do not drive under the influence of alcohol or drugs.
  • Reckless driving and overtaking at blind curves is prohibited.
  • Do not park in no-parking zones or obstruct traffic.
  • Racing or performing stunts on public roads is illegal.
  • Vehicles emitting excessive smoke or noise pollution are not allowed.

üìú Driving License Regulations

  • All drivers must carry a valid driving license at all times.
  • New drivers must complete a probation period of one year.
  • Commercial drivers must undergo periodic medical fitness tests.
  • Driving without a valid license is punishable by law.
  • Renewal of a driving license must be done every five years.

üìÑ Vehicle Registration and Insurance

  • All vehicles must be registered with the Department of Transport Management.
  • Vehicle owners must renew their registration annually.
  • Third-party insurance is mandatory for all motor vehicles.
  • Vehicles without proper registration and insurance will be impounded.
  • Number plates must be clearly visible at all times.

üí∏ Penalties

  • Drunk Driving: NPR 5000 fine.
  • Speeding: NPR 1500 fine.
  • Red Light Violation: NPR 1000 fine.
  • No Helmet: NPR 500 fine.
  • Driving Without a License: NPR 2000 fine.
  • Overloading Passengers or Goods: NPR 3000 fine.
  • Using a Mobile Phone While Driving: NPR 1500 fine.
